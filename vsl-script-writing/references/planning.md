## Product Details

1. **Product Name**: What is it called?
2. **Product Function**: What does it do? Describe it in one sentence.
3. **Price**: What is the main price point?
4. **Discounts**: Are there any discounts, bundles, or special pricing? (e.g., buy 2 get 1 free, founding member pricing)
5. **Bonuses**: What bonuses come with the product? List each with its standalone value.
6. **Unique Mechanism Name**: What is the proprietary system, method, ingredient, or process called? (e.g., "The Metabolic Flip," "pH Buffered Creatine," "The Profit Path Accelerator"). If it doesn't have one, create one.

## Right Customer / Wrong Customer

**Right Customer** — Describe the ideal buyer. Who benefits most from this product?
- Demographics (age, gender, income, occupation)
- Psychographics (beliefs, values, lifestyle)
- Current situation (what they're dealing with right now)
- What they've already tried that hasn't worked

**Wrong Customer** — Who should NOT buy this? Being specific about who it's NOT for increases trust and makes the right customers lean in harder.

## Goal, Enemy, Hero

**Goal**: What is the single biggest transformation or outcome your product delivers?

**Enemy**: What force is working against your prospect? This could be:
- A villain (big pharma, Wall Street, the government, an industry)
- A myth (a widely-believed lie that's keeping them stuck)
- A hidden problem (something they don't even know is sabotaging them)
- A broken system (the old way of doing things that doesn't work)

**Hero**: Who is the guide/expert presenting the solution? This is usually the founder, creator, or spokesperson. What makes them credible? What is their personal connection to the problem?

## Proof of Concept

Answer these five questions to build your credibility arsenal:

### 1. Do you have credibility to establish?
- Expert credentials, years of experience, media appearances
- Awards, certifications, institutional affiliations
- Number of customers served, products sold
- Notable clients or partnerships

### 2. Can you visually demonstrate proof?
- Before/after photos or results
- Screenshots of results (dashboards, bank statements, analytics)
- Product demonstrations or lab results
- Charts, graphs, or data visualizations
- Supplement facts labels, ingredient breakdowns

### 3. Can you prove it's fast, easy, and safe?
- Speed: How quickly do users see results?
- Ease: How simple is the implementation?
- Safety: What makes this risk-free? Any clinical backing?

### 4. Can you help them avoid future regret?
- What happens if they DON'T act? (cost of inaction)
- What will they miss out on?
- How will the problem get worse over time?

### 5. What testimonials, case studies, or social proof do you have?
- Customer results with specific numbers
- Expert endorsements
- Media mentions or press coverage
- Community size (Facebook fans, email subscribers, customers)
- Star ratings, reviews

## Reasons Why

Answer these five critical questions. These form the logical backbone of your VSL:

### 1. Why should they buy this type of product at all?
What makes this category of solution the right approach?

### 2. Why should they NOT buy from a competitor?
What's wrong with the alternatives? What do competitors get wrong?

### 3. Why should they buy from YOUR company specifically?
What's your unique advantage? Story? Mission? Track record?

### 4. Why should they buy RIGHT NOW?
What's the urgency? Price going up? Limited supply? Seasonal relevance? Problem getting worse?

### 5. Why might they NOT buy — and what's your answer to each objection?
List every objection and your rebuttal:
- Price objection: _______________
- Skepticism objection: _______________
- "Not for me" objection: _______________
- Timing objection: _______________
- Trust objection: _______________

## Epiphany Bridge Story

This is the personal story that bridges the gap between the problem and the solution. It's NOT a lecture — it's a vulnerable, relatable narrative.

1. **Your Backstory**: What was your situation before discovering the solution?
2. **The Wall**: What moment of failure or frustration made you realize the old way wasn't working?
3. **The Epiphany**: What was the "aha!" moment that changed everything?
4. **The New Solution**: How did that epiphany lead to creating this product?
5. **The Results**: What happened when you (or others) used it?

**Key**: The viewer should feel like they're having the epiphany WITH you, not being told about it.

## Tone & Style

What should the tone of the VSL be?
- Energetic and hyped
- Serious and professional
- Empathetic and caring
- Urgent and fear-based
- Casual and conversational
- Authoritative and expert-driven

**Voice guidance**: Write like you talk. Short sentences. Conversational language. No jargon unless your audience uses it. The goal is connection, not sounding smart.

---

## BDF Quick-Check (Beliefs, Desires, Fears)

Before moving to writing, verify you can answer:
- **Beliefs**: What does your prospect already believe about their problem, existing solutions, and the world?
- **Desires**: What do they want most? What outcome are they seeking?
- **Fears**: What are they afraid of? What keeps them up at night?

If you can't answer these with specific, vivid detail, you need more research before writing.
